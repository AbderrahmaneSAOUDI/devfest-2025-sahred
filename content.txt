        DevFest is the world's largest community-driven tech conference, bringing together
          passionate developers from around the globe. Hosted annually by Google Developer
          Groups, DevFest offers a unique opportunity to explore developer tools, learn from
          Google and Google Developer Experts, and connect with fellow developers.
        
        Last year’s DevFest centered on Responsible AI. This theme allowed for exploration
          of the importance of developing technologies that enhance productivity responsibly,
          ensuring AI serves humanity.
        
        We're excited to reveal the theme for this year!
        DevFest 2025: Building Safe, Secure and Scalable Solutions with AI and Cloud.
        This year, DevFest 2025 will empower developer communities worldwide to architect the
          future. We'll focus on driving the practical integration of AI and Google Cloud
          into their solutions. Our goal is to equip developers with the skills to build
          innovative, scalable, and ethically sound applications, acknowledging their
          pivotal role in shaping a technology-driven future that benefits everyone.

Best practices for workshops at DevFest: 
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Separate workshop RSVPs
        
          To streamline your planning, secure necessary resources like Cloud Credits, and
             ensure a smoother overall event experience, you have two primary options for
             managing workshop registrations:
             
               Create separate registration processes for workshops as individual events.
                   This is ideal if workshops have distinct audiences, happen on a different day
                   then the main DevFest event or require significant independent planning.
               
               Implement multiple RSVP options within a single Bevy event. This
                 approach keeps all related registrations under one umbrella while still allowing
                 attendees to sign up for specific workshops.

                 

                 Note: If using external registration platforms, be mindful of
                 potential increased costs associated with higher registration numbers. Plan
                 accordingly to avoid unexpected expenses.
               
             
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Consider a friendly user experience for embedded workshops
        
          If workshops are integrated into the main event agenda, carefully consider how to
            manage registration without disrupting the user's journey on the website. The
            process should be intuitive and easy to navigate!
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Use tagging
        
          Utilize tags for workshops or covered technologies as part of the overall event tags.
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Pre-DevFest workshops
        
          Explore hosting “Road to DevFest” workshop events prior to the main DevFest. This
             can build anticipation and provide a dedicated learning experience.
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Structured event timelines
        
          Consider splitting a multi-day DevFest (e.g., one day for talks, one day for
             workshops) or organizing the daily schedule with morning workshops and afternoon
             talks to optimize engagement and learning.
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Beyond the basics: Hands-on & thematic learning!
        
          To have a more engaging experience, consider incorporating hackathons. These
             provide an excellent opportunity to apply newly acquired skills in a practical
             setting. Explore thematic approaches for workshops, such as "tech for good"
             initiatives or workshops focused on solving local problems.

 Here are some best practices for you to take into consideration while you plan your DevFest: For planning your DevFest event: 
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Start early
        
          Planning a DevFest is an exciting undertaking, and getting an early start sets you
             up for success! We recommend giving yourself at least three months to thoroughly
             plan, gather feedback, and brainstorm all your brilliant ideas.
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Secure your venue (or platform!) early
        
          DevFests are often most vibrant when experienced in person! However, we fully
             support hosting a virtual or hybrid event if that's what best meets your
             community's needs and preferences. The important thing is to finalize your venue
             plans well in advance to ensure a successful event.
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        
            Publish your event on the GDG Event Platform and use the “DevFest” tag
            
          
        
          Why? Publish on GDG Event Platform
             will give you the opportunity to get your event surfaced on the DevFest website.
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Find volunteers
        
          Everyone is excited for DevFest. Encourage some of your community members or GDG on
             Campus in your area to help and get involved!
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Facilitators and technical set up matter
        
          Planning a codelab or practical session? These are brilliant for attendee engagement!
             To make sure your session runs flawlessly, focus on a solid technical setup (think
             strong WiFi, adequate desks/tables, and a good presenter screen). Also, ensure you
             have dedicated facilitators on hand who can quickly troubleshoot any issues. This
             preparation makes all the difference!
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Get sponsors for your event
        
          Sponsorship is a great way to boost your event's budget. To find sponsors, start by
             identifying companies whose target audience aligns with your event's attendees and
             DevFest 2025 theme. Create a professional proposal that outlines different
             sponsorship tiers with clear benefits. Focus on the value you can offer sponsors,
             such as brand exposure or lead generation, and personalize your approach to show
             why their partnership is a great fit for your event.
          
          To make your proposal even more appealing, consider those:
            
              Access to a targeted audience: Emphasize high attendance numbers to
                show sponsors they can reach potential customers and recruits.
              
              Speaking opportunities: Offer dedicated slots for sponsors to showcase
                their expertise.
              
              Recruitment & job fair: Attract sponsors by providing an on-site job
                fair where they can offer positions and even conduct interviews.
              
              In-kind sponsorships: Accept non-monetary contributions like a venue,
                catering, or other services to reduce your event costs.
              
              Attendee benefits: Offer appealing perks for attendees, such as
                vouchers for training or certifications, that are provided by the sponsor.
              
              Brand alignment: Highlight the positive impact and strong community
                engagement of DevFest, allowing sponsors to align their brand with a reputable
                and trusted event.
              
            
          
        
      
    
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Craft a smart agenda
        
          Plan an agenda that is flexible and engaging from the very beginning. To ensure a
             full room for your key speakers, schedule networking or welcome activities at the
             start of your event. This buffer period gives attendees time to arrive, check in,
             and get settled without missing important content. It also creates a more relaxed
             and social atmosphere, making your event a better experience for everyone.
          
        
      
    
  
  
    
      
        
          
          keyboard_arrow_up
        Request attendees feedback right at the event
        
          Getting feedback from attendees can be a challenge, but we have a tip that can
             significantly increase your response rate: ask for it while they're still there.
          
          If you use Bevy to register your event, feedback forms are sent automatically from
             Bevy to your event attendees 15 minutes before your scheduled event ends. Ask people
             to take the survey while they are still in the room: At the very end of your final
             session, put up a slide asking attendees to check their phones for the survey and fill
             it out on the spot. Make sure the surveys got sent already!
          
          If your event finishes with a networking time or a happy hour:
            
              Make it Visible: Display a slide on screens or monitors. Include a message
                like: “Check your inbox- will in the feedback survey!” QR code that links directly
                to the feedback form, along with a simple message like, "Help us improve! Scan here
                to share your feedback."
              
              Use Your Team: Ask a few staff members to briefly mention the feedback
                form while they're mingling. A friendly, personal reminder like, "Hey, if you have
                two minutes, the feedback link is in your inbox and the chat. It really helps us
                out," can make a big difference.
              
            
          
          This timing is key. By having attendees complete the survey before they leave, you'll
            capture their thoughts and opinions while they're still fresh!

      What is unique about DevFest?
      
        What makes DevFest unique is its combined focus on community, learning, and Google technologies. Unlike a typical developer meetup or talk, DevFest events are larger affairs that offer a wider range of activities: 
   Multi-faceted learning: DevFest goes beyond just talks. Expect workshops, codelabs, and other hands-on sessions that allow attendees to dive deeper into specific topics.
   Local twist: Because they're organized by Google Developer Groups, DevFests can incorporate local flavors and languages into the program, making them relevant to the community they serve.
   Global moment: Despite the local focus, DevFests happen all over the world with hundreds of Google Developer Groups creating a space for their communities to learn, connect and celebrate together.

      How can GDG on Campus participate?
      
        To best unite the local developer community, it is advisable to avoid multiple groups hosting DevFests. While GDGs will continue organizing DevFests, GDGs on Campus can offer support and guidance to the Google Developer Groups responsible for these events. We are committed to fostering connections and collaboration for optimal event experiences. 
  Support and consulting: GDG on Campus organizers will offer support to Google Developer Groups, who are responsible for hosting DevFests.
  Collaboration: While regional teams can facilitate communication and strengthen relationships, collaboration ultimately depends on the communities themselves.
  Participation: Members of the GDG on Campus  community are welcome to attend their local DevFests.
