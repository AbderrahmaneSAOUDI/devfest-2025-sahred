Google Developer Groups

Google Developer Groups (GDGs) foster thriving communities where developers connect, learn, and grow together. With over 1000+ groups in 145+ countries, GDGs are the largest network of developers in the world. Each local GDG provides a space for professionals to share their passion for Google technologies and build successful projects.

Need support? Reach out to us at gdg-support@google.com.
For region-specific questions, please contact your Regional Lead.

New to GDG

Who are organizers?

Organizers of Google Developer Groups are passionate leaders in their community who are dedicated to helping others learn and connect. GDG organizers plan and host meetup events on a wide range of technical topics typically on a monthly basis in a location near them. These community organizers come with various backgrounds, from all types of companies and industries.

GDG Learning Pathways

GDG learning pathway is a global educational tool that teaches the basics of running the GDG community and empowers organizers to build strong communities. Through short videos and Codelabs, you will learn what it means to be a GDG organizer, how to manage and promote your communities, and navigate all tools and platforms. Once you complete a quiz, you will get a badge to prove your knowledge.

IMPORTANT: Before you get started with your new role as GDG Organizer, please ensure you’ve set your profile on the GDG Event Platform using a Google Account and reviewed the GDG Terms & Conditions.


Benefits for you

Grow professionally

  • The largest professional developer community and network.
  • New skills from creating, fostering, and leading a community of developers.
  • Community-management training and invitations to events hosted by Google Developers.
  • Technical content and training materials curated by product teams at Google, which give you a deeper understanding of Google's products and platforms.

Help other developers learn and grow

  • Help other developers learn and grow by sharing your knowledge and expertise.
  • Mentor those who are new to the field, guiding them through challenges and celebrating their successes.
  • Help other developers build their networks by connecting them with events and opportunities.
  • Cultivate a culture of continuous learning within the local developer community.

Grow your network

  • Grow your network by joining an international community of like-minded leaders.
  • Connect with developers, industry experts, and Googlers from all over the world.
  • Gain new perspectives and discover a diverse range of opportunities.
  • Expand your professional circle and build valuable, long-lasting relationships.

What is expected from you

In short, you're expected to build an inclusive community where professional developers from the local area meet, connect, learn, and grow.

These guidelines and code of conduct cover both online (e.g. mailing lists, social channels) and offline (e.g. in-person events) behaviors. Add it to your website and have it visible during the event as well.

New Chapter

Get a grasp on your local developer ecosystem. Ask yourself:

  • What other communities in your area support technologists?
  • What does the enterprise or startup ecosystem look like?
  • Are there a lot of universities?
  • What type of opportunities are around?

Figure out who your developers are. Ask yourself:

  • Who makes up your community?
  • Who are the developers in your city?
  • What are they interested in?

Existing Chapter

Meet with the current Organizers:

Understand the chapter's vision and purpose, the experience of developers in the community, and what has and hasn't worked.

Collect feedback:

Collect feedback from chapter members to understand their experience, the role the developer community plays for them, and how you and the other Organizers can improve their experience.

Chapter activity

What does this mean?

  • After 90 days of inactivity, you will receive periodic reminders with updates on your community status and resources that may help with event planning.
  • After 180 days of inactivity, your chapter is considered “inactive” and your chapter will no longer be searchable and visible to users on the GDG event platform as well as, your chapter members. Don’t worry - you can still create amazing events. Your chapter team will still have access and once you host an event, your chapter will become active and be accessible on the GDG event platform.
  • After 273 days of inactivity, we will assume that you do not want to be part of the program, and your chapter and organizing team will be removed from the GDG program and you will have to apply to create a new chapter.

There are over 1000 GDGs across the globe and our hope is that each GDG is actively helping local developers learn and grow. That being said, being a GDG organizer can be challenging so, please reach out if you have any blockers or need support.
