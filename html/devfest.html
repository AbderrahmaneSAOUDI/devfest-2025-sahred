DevFest is the world's largest community-driven tech conference, bringing together passionate developers from around the globe. Hosted annually by Google Developer Groups, DevFest offers a unique opportunity to explore developer tools, learn from Google and Google Developer Experts, and connect with fellow developers.

Last year’s DevFest centered on Responsible AI. This theme allowed for exploration of the importance of developing technologies that enhance productivity responsibly, ensuring AI serves humanity.

We're excited to reveal the theme for this year!

DevFest 2025: Building Safe, Secure and Scalable Solutions with AI and Cloud.

This year, DevFest 2025 will empower developer communities worldwide to architect the future. We'll focus on driving the practical integration of AI and Google Cloud into their solutions. Our goal is to equip developers with the skills to build innovative, scalable, and ethically sound applications, acknowledging their pivotal role in shaping a technology-driven future that benefits everyone.

Organizer checklist

  1. Create - Create your event on the GDG event platform:
    1. By using the DevFest tag you’ll have the opportunity to get your event featured on our DevFest website once it launches: goo.gle/devfest
    2. External ticketing? If you are collecting registrations on a different page, no worries! Use the external ticketing event type and we’ll reach out to you to get them added into the event into the GDG Event Platform.
    3. You can request event support here whether your event is published or still in planning.
    4. To get connected with GDEs and Googlers, you will need to build a Call for Participation (CFP) in Advocu. Learn more here.
    5. Remember to include event details such as speakers, agenda, and sponsors. This information helps attendees know what to expect and allows us to track your event.
  2. Host - Host your DevFest, have fun, take photos and videos, and share them on your socials using #DevFest
    1. Pro-tip: Consider using the DevFest Opener Slides Template for your event. This optional template provides the opening slides, and you are encouraged to localize and expand the deck to meet the specific needs and interests of your local community. Use it to share vital, up-to-date information on DevFest, the Google Developer Groups program, and relevant Google tools.
  3. Report - Show your impact by completing the post-event team survey.

Best practices for workshops at DevFest:

  1. Separate workshop RSVPs

    keyboard_arrow_up

    To streamline your planning, secure necessary resources like Cloud Credits, and ensure a smoother overall event experience, you have two primary options for managing workshop registrations:

    • Create separate registration processes for workshops as individual events. This is ideal if workshops have distinct audiences, happen on a different day then the main DevFest event or require significant independent planning.
    • Implement multiple RSVP options within a single Bevy event. This approach keeps all related registrations under one umbrella while still allowing attendees to sign up for specific workshops.

      Note: If using external registration platforms, be mindful of potential increased costs associated with higher registration numbers. Plan accordingly to avoid unexpected expenses.

  2. Consider a friendly user experience for embedded workshops

    keyboard_arrow_up

    If workshops are integrated into the main event agenda, carefully consider how to manage registration without disrupting the user's journey on the website. The process should be intuitive and easy to navigate!

  3. Use tagging

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Utilize tags for workshops or covered technologies as part of the overall event tags.

  4. Pre-DevFest workshops

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Explore hosting “Road to DevFest” workshop events prior to the main DevFest. This can build anticipation and provide a dedicated learning experience.

  5. Structured event timelines

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Consider splitting a multi-day DevFest (e.g., one day for talks, one day for workshops) or organizing the daily schedule with morning workshops and afternoon talks to optimize engagement and learning.

  6. Beyond the basics: Hands-on & thematic learning!

    keyboard_arrow_up

    To have a more engaging experience, consider incorporating hackathons. These provide an excellent opportunity to apply newly acquired skills in a practical setting. Explore thematic approaches for workshops, such as "tech for good" initiatives or workshops focused on solving local problems.

Best practices

Here are some best practices for you to take into consideration while you plan your DevFest:

For planning your DevFest event:

  1. Start early

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Planning a DevFest is an exciting undertaking, and getting an early start sets you up for success! We recommend giving yourself at least three months to thoroughly plan, gather feedback, and brainstorm all your brilliant ideas.

  2. Secure your venue (or platform!) early

    keyboard_arrow_up

    DevFests are often most vibrant when experienced in person! However, we fully support hosting a virtual or hybrid event if that's what best meets your community's needs and preferences. The important thing is to finalize your venue plans well in advance to ensure a successful event.

  3. Publish your event on the GDG Event Platform and use the “DevFest” tag

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Why? Publish on GDG Event Platform will give you the opportunity to get your event surfaced on the DevFest website.

  4. Find volunteers

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Everyone is excited for DevFest. Encourage some of your community members or GDG on Campus in your area to help and get involved!

  5. Facilitators and technical set up matter

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Planning a codelab or practical session? These are brilliant for attendee engagement! To make sure your session runs flawlessly, focus on a solid technical setup (think strong WiFi, adequate desks/tables, and a good presenter screen). Also, ensure you have dedicated facilitators on hand who can quickly troubleshoot any issues. This preparation makes all the difference!

  6. Get sponsors for your event

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Sponsorship is a great way to boost your event's budget. To find sponsors, start by identifying companies whose target audience aligns with your event's attendees and DevFest 2025 theme. Create a professional proposal that outlines different sponsorship tiers with clear benefits. Focus on the value you can offer sponsors, such as brand exposure or lead generation, and personalize your approach to show why their partnership is a great fit for your event.

    To make your proposal even more appealing, consider those:

    • Access to a targeted audience: Emphasize high attendance numbers to show sponsors they can reach potential customers and recruits.
    • Speaking opportunities: Offer dedicated slots for sponsors to showcase their expertise.
    • Recruitment & job fair: Attract sponsors by providing an on-site job fair where they can offer positions and even conduct interviews.
    • In-kind sponsorships: Accept non-monetary contributions like a venue, catering, or other services to reduce your event costs.
    • Attendee benefits: Offer appealing perks for attendees, such as vouchers for training or certifications, that are provided by the sponsor.
    • Brand alignment: Highlight the positive impact and strong community engagement of DevFest, allowing sponsors to align their brand with a reputable and trusted event.

  7. Craft a smart agenda

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Plan an agenda that is flexible and engaging from the very beginning. To ensure a full room for your key speakers, schedule networking or welcome activities at the start of your event. This buffer period gives attendees time to arrive, check in, and get settled without missing important content. It also creates a more relaxed and social atmosphere, making your event a better experience for everyone.

  8. Request attendees feedback right at the event

    keyboard_arrow_up

    Getting feedback from attendees can be a challenge, but we have a tip that can significantly increase your response rate: ask for it while they're still there.

    If you use Bevy to register your event, feedback forms are sent automatically from Bevy to your event attendees 15 minutes before your scheduled event ends. Ask people to take the survey while they are still in the room: At the very end of your final session, put up a slide asking attendees to check their phones for the survey and fill it out on the spot. Make sure the surveys got sent already!

    If your event finishes with a networking time or a happy hour:

    • Make it Visible: Display a slide on screens or monitors. Include a message like: “Check your inbox- will in the feedback survey!” QR code that links directly to the feedback form, along with a simple message like, "Help us improve! Scan here to share your feedback."
    • Use Your Team: Ask a few staff members to briefly mention the feedback form while they're mingling. A friendly, personal reminder like, "Hey, if you have two minutes, the feedback link is in your inbox and the chat. It really helps us out," can make a big difference.

    This timing is key. By having attendees complete the survey before they leave, you'll capture their thoughts and opinions while they're still fresh!

DevFest FAQs

If you do not find the answer to your question, reach out to your Regional Lead or to gdg-support@google.com.

What is unique about DevFest?

keyboard_arrow_up

What makes DevFest unique is its combined focus on community, learning, and Google technologies. Unlike a typical developer meetup or talk, DevFest events are larger affairs that offer a wider range of activities:

  • Multi-faceted learning: DevFest goes beyond just talks. Expect workshops, codelabs, and other hands-on sessions that allow attendees to dive deeper into specific topics.
  • Local twist: Because they're organized by Google Developer Groups, DevFests can incorporate local flavors and languages into the program, making them relevant to the community they serve.
  • Global moment: Despite the local focus, DevFests happen all over the world with hundreds of Google Developer Groups creating a space for their communities to learn, connect and celebrate together.

What are "topic" tags?

keyboard_arrow_up

We recommend adding the DevFest tag when you create your event in the GDG Event Platform. This helps inform attendees of what topics will be covered at your event and informs the Google team of how we can support your event.

How can I get Experts to my event?

keyboard_arrow_up

GDG organizers and Google Developer Experts can now streamline their collaboration with the speaker matching process in Advocu. On the platform, you can also find Googlers.

Learn More.

Why should I host a DevFest event for my community?

keyboard_arrow_up

Hosting a DevFest will help your community connect and engage. This is a unique opportunity to gather your local developers in the biggest tech conference of the year.

How can GDG on Campus participate?

keyboard_arrow_up

To best unite the local developer community, it is advisable to avoid multiple groups hosting DevFests. While GDGs will continue organizing DevFests, GDGs on Campus can offer support and guidance to the Google Developer Groups responsible for these events. We are committed to fostering connections and collaboration for optimal event experiences.

  • Support and consulting: GDG on Campus organizers will offer support to Google Developer Groups, who are responsible for hosting DevFests.
  • Collaboration: While regional teams can facilitate communication and strengthen relationships, collaboration ultimately depends on the communities themselves.
  • Participation: Members of the GDG on Campus community are welcome to attend their local DevFests.

Why do we use the GDG Event Platform?

keyboard_arrow_up

The GDG Event Platform is designed to streamline your group's membership management and event organization. By diligently publishing your events with accurate tags and details, you can enhance discoverability, simplify event management, and receive valuable data insights, all while ensuring a consistent and trusted branding experience for the global developer community.

Beyond data collection, you'll also find helpful features like waitlist management, attendee surveying, and newsletter distribution, all designed to enhance your event organization and attendee engagement.
