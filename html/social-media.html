Share your stories!

Google Developers is seeking stories about members of Google Developer Groups. Submit success stories about your community at goo.gle/communitystory. Stories selected will be highlighted in a short video, blog, newsletter, and/or featured social media posts.

Check out recently published GDG stories at developers.google.com/community/gdg/stories.

Sharing stories helps inspire others. It allows us to learn from and celebrate impactful work in the developer community and even the building of great things. Let us recognize some of these amazing efforts!

We’re particularly interested in stories with the following themes:

  • Regional challenges being addressed by a local developer
  • Product development using Google technologies
  • Career growth due to technical learning
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Social change
  • Mentorship
  • Inclusivity
  • Accessibility
  • Noteworthy speakers or attendees

How to introduce GDG to potential members and sponsors

Use the official Google Developers community video to introduce GDG to potential members or sponsors.

Share these in an email to your network, present it at the start of a meetup event, or post it to your social media channels.

To showcase the value of GDG, you can mention the variety of events like hands-on workshops, expert-led talks, and hackathons that empower members to build and innovate with Google technologies.

Additionally, you can mention different success stories or known testimonials coming from your chapter and its members.

GDG Press: How to get local press articles about your GDG

Did you know that the local press love to cover stories on community impact? Local press (e.g. tech blogs, websites, newspapers) are a great way to promote your DevFest and community. Here are steps you can take.

  1. Research and identify local media outlets that write about local community opportunities and success.
  2. Find the contact online for the relevant press publication
  3. Send a brief summary explaining your virtual conference
  4. Where/when
  5. Event focus
  6. Why your conference is important
  7. Interesting developer success story from your community
  8. Special keynote speakers
  9. If you get any requests from the press to interview someone at Google working on the program, please feel free to direct the reporter to press@google.com.
  10. If you think there is a story/angle that could be of interest to the press that would require an interview or extends beyond your event, submit the story lead at developers.google.com/community/gdg/stories.

Here's a sample email template you can use when reaching out to press:

Hi there,

My name is [insert name] and I’m a developer community organizer for Google Developer Groups [location]. GDGs are community-led meetups for those who are interested in learning about Google technologies and DevFest is our flagship annual event.

We are hoping to inspire your readers around the power of community.

Our community has [x] members and we hope to grow even further!

[Inspiring story of one of your community members]

Can we set up some time to speak before our next virtual meetup?

You can learn more at [link] or our local event page [link].

We aim to bring together those with shared interests in not only learning about things like Cloud, Android, Machine Learning, AI and more, but support one another in our growth, make connections and even build together!

Hope to hear from you soon.

[name]
